# Chapter 1 Sketch

Two threads run through this chapter: the inner voice (develops with exploration) and the environment (modular, explored freely).

No choices until after waking. Everything before that is pure prose.

---

## THE OPENING (linear)

### 1. The Corridor

Dark, stone on both sides. Bones underfoot — hollow, dry, thinning as the floor rises. A glance back into ponderous dark with no end and no origin. Ahead: the walls pull apart, the ceiling lifts, grey light without a source. The hum rising through stone.

A hollow in the wall worn to the shape of a body. Warm. Sleep.

### 2. Waking

Grey light. A small chamber, curved, crusted with soot and grease. The hollow still warm. On the left forearm — the bracer. Dark metal, close-fitted, channels and recesses. The room is waiting.

The hub opens.

---

## THE ENVIRONMENT (modular blocks)

These can be explored in any order after waking.

### The Bracer
Inspect the bracer — always available. Inspection text appears in the right panel. The text changes as engravings accumulate. Early: empty channels, warmth, the sense of something waiting. After fire: new lines. After the killing challenge: deeper engraving.

### The Ledge (flint, steel, tinder)
Objects in a niche, worn smooth from use. Dark stone, grey metal bar, dry fibres.

**Micro challenge: Making fire**
- Strike stone on metal → sparks die on stone. Returns to choice.
- Arrange fibres then strike → fire. Light fills the chamber. Bracer changes — new lines appear.
- Take them and move on → passage won't open. Must return.

### The Basin
Shallow depression in the floor. Dry before fire. After fire: water wells up. She drinks. The Torus's first transaction.

### The Wall Marks
Layers of scratches, handprints, tally marks. Among them, the first litany (Gendlin). Optional deeper exploration: tally marks (counting days?), a small handprint, a crude drawing.

### The Passage
Opening at the far end. Air moves through it. Before fire engraving: dead end after a few steps. After fire engraving: bracer responds, passage extends. Access to the killing challenge.

---

## THE KILLING CHALLENGE

### The Balance Room

A different space. Angular, high ceiling. A beam balance on a pedestal, connected to the way forward. Left dish: four dark stones. Right dish: empty. On the shelf: dark and pale stones.

Scratched on the wall: **⚫⚫ = ⚪**

**Choices:**
1. Four dark stones → beam levels, nothing happens, resets (equivalence ≠ identity)
2. Two pale stones → beam levels, way opens, bracer engraving deepens (success)
3. One pale stone → beam tips left, resets (too light)
4. Three pale stones → ceiling comes down, death, restart chapter

**Concept:** Substitution and equivalence. 2 dark = 1 pale. Therefore 4 dark = 2 pale.

Beyond: the passage curves away. The walls slightly cleaner. Slightly less worn.

## CHAPTER SUMMARY

- ~2000-3000 words total
- Linear opening: corridor, hollow, sleep, waking
- Free exploration after waking: bracer, flint, basin, wall marks, passage
- One micro challenge: making fire (engraving reward, unlocks basin and passage)
- One litany: Gendlin
- One killing challenge: the balance puzzle (substitution/equivalence)
- Concepts taught: fire as transaction, equivalence vs identity, substitution
