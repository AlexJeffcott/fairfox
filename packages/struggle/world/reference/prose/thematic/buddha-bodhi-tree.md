# The Buddha Under the Bodhi Tree

Ashvaghosha's *Buddhacarita* gives the fullest account of the night. Siddhartha sits beneath the tree and resolves not to rise until he has seen through to the end of things. Mara, lord of desire and death, comes to stop him. First he sends his armies — demons with the faces of beasts, carrying trees torn up by the roots, hurling fire and stones and darkness. Ashvaghosha describes the scene: *"Some stood in the sky, some on the tops of trees; some tore up the earth, some flew about in all directions."* Siddhartha does not move. Then Mara sends his daughters — Desire, Pleasure, Restlessness — to seduce him. Siddhartha does not move. Mara challenges his right to sit there at all: who are you to claim this seat? Siddhartha touches the earth with his right hand, and the earth itself testifies for him. This is the *bhumisparsha mudra*, the earth-touching gesture — the most depicted moment in all of Buddhist art. The Pali texts describe what follows in the three watches of the night. In the first watch he sees all his own previous lives, stretching back through incalculable ages. In the second watch he sees the deaths and rebirths of all beings, *"passing away and being reborn, low and high, bright and dark, in happy or unhappy states according to their karma."* In the third watch he sees the chain of dependent origination — the twelve links by which ignorance gives rise to suffering — and sees through it. The *Majjhima Nikaya* gives his own words at dawn: *"My mind was emancipated… Ignorance was dispelled, knowledge arose; darkness was dispelled, light arose — as happens in one who abides diligent, ardent, and resolute."* The phrasing is almost clinical. Darkness was dispelled. Light arose. He says it as though describing weather. The night of Mara's assault, with all its cosmic violence, resolves into a sentence of perfect calm. That is the point. The struggle was real, but the one who sat through it did not struggle. He stayed still, and let the darkness exhaust itself against him.
