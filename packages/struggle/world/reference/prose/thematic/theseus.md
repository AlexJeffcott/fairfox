# Theseus

The labyrinth at Knossos was built to be inescapable. Minos commanded Daedalus to construct it — *"tecti turbata per ambages"*, as Ovid puts it, "confused by the winding passages of the enclosure" — a structure of such bewildering intricacy that its own maker could barely find the exit (*Met.* VIII.161–168). At its centre waited the Minotaur. Theseus sailed voluntarily among the youths sent as tribute, and he could kill the beast — that was the lesser problem. The greater one was the dark itself, the turning corridors that folded back on themselves until direction lost all meaning. Catullus dwells on this: the *"flexuous curves of the blind walls"* from which no one returned (64.112–115). The killing is almost incidental. What matters is what came before and after it — the thread.

Plutarch gives us the moment clearly. Ariadne, "having fallen in love with Theseus at the sight of him," went to Daedalus, and the old craftsman gave her a ball of thread — the *clew* — so that Theseus could tie one end at the entrance and unwind it as he walked into the dark. That slender line was the only thing connecting him to the world above. Ovid says he made his way back *"curva recurvans / errabunda regens tenui vestigia filo"* — "guiding his wandering steps through the winding ways by means of the slender thread" (*Met.* VIII.172–173). *Tenui filo.* A thin thread. Catullus makes the same point: Theseus emerged only by *"directing his footsteps by a fine thread, lest the inextricable wandering of the labyrinth should defeat him as he retraced its passage"* (*ne labyrintheis e flexibus egredientem / tecti frustraretur inobservabilis error*, 64.113–115). The word that recurs across every telling is *tenuis* — thin, fine, almost nothing. Strength got him to the centre. Only the thread got him out. And the thread was not his. It was given to him by someone who loved him, devised by someone who had built the trap in the first place. The labyrinth could not be solved by courage or force or even cleverness. It could only be solved by a connection maintained, step by step, back to the light — a single fragile line held taut through every blind turning, never let go.
