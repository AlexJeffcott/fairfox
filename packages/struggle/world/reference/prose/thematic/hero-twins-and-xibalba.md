# The Hero Twins and Xibalba

The *Popol Vuh* tells the story twice — first as tragedy, then as triumph. The father and uncle of the Hero Twins, One Hunahpu and Seven Hunahpu, are summoned to Xibalba, the underworld, by its lords. They fail every test and are sacrificed. One Hunahpu's severed head is hung in a calabash tree, where it spits into the hand of a passing maiden and impregnates her. The Hero Twins, Hunahpu and Xbalanque, are born above and grow up knowing what waits below. When the lords of Xibalba summon them in turn, they go — but they go prepared. The *Popol Vuh* names the houses of torment: *"the first is called Dark House, where there is only darkness within."* Then Razor House, *"in which the blades shifted and clattered inside."* Then Cold House. Then Jaguar House. Then Fire House. Then Bat House, *"filled with screeching bats, great bats shut up within the house."* The first pair of brothers failed these houses. The Twins survive them through cunning — they burn torches that are not really torches, offer sacrifices that are not really sacrifices. When at last the lords of death kill them and grind their bones and cast them into the river, the Twins return, reborn as catfish, then as wandering performers. They dance before the lords of Xibalba and perform miracles: *"They sacrificed themselves, one of them dying, stretched out as if dead. Instantly he came back to life. The Xibalbans were fascinated."* The lords demand the trick be performed on them. The Twins oblige — and do not bring them back. *"And such was the defeat of the lords of Xibalba. It was only through wonders and through their self-transformation that they accomplished it."* The Twins did not fight their way out. They let themselves be killed, came back, and tricked death into volunteering for its own destruction.
