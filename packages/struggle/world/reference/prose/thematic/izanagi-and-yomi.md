# Izanagi and Yomi

The *Kojiki*, Japan's oldest chronicle, tells a version of this story that mirrors Orpheus so closely it seems to belong to some shared grammar of grief. Izanagi's wife Izanami dies giving birth to the god of fire, and Izanagi follows her to Yomi, the land of the dead. He finds her in the darkness and speaks to her: *"My beloved spouse, the lands that you and I were making have not yet been completed. You must come back."* Izanami answers from the dark: *"How I regret that you did not come sooner. I have eaten of the food of Yomi."* She has eaten the food of the dead — the same binding that holds Persephone — and she cannot simply leave. She tells Izanagi she will ask the gods of Yomi to release her, but he must not look at her. *"Do not look upon me,"* she says, and withdraws. He waits. He cannot bear the waiting. He breaks a tooth from the comb that binds his hair, lights it as a torch, and enters the hall where she lies — and sees her. *"Maggots swarmed and roared in her body. In her head was Great-Thunder. In her breast was Fire-Thunder. In her belly was Black-Thunder."* He flees in horror. Izanami, betrayed and furious, sends the hags of Yomi to pursue him: the Ugly Females of Yomi, then the thunder gods, then a host of warriors of the dead, one thousand five hundred strong. Izanagi hurls behind him first his headdress, which turns into grapes; then his comb, which turns into bamboo shoots — and the creatures of death stop to eat them. He reaches the pass between the living and the dead and blocks it with a boulder *"that would require a thousand men to move."* Izanami calls out from the other side: *"My beloved husband, if you do this, I will each day strangle to death one thousand of the people of your land."* Izanagi answers: *"My beloved wife, if you do this, I will each day build one thousand five hundred birthing huts."* The marriage ends across a sealed stone, with a vow of mutual destruction. Izanagi escaped. But the door between the worlds is shut forever, and the cost of his looking was the end of everything they had built together.
