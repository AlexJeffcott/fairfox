# Rumi — Opening of the Masnavi

---

*Listen to the reed, how it tells a tale, complaining of separations — saying, "Ever since I was parted from the reedbed, my lament has caused man and woman to moan."*

*I want a bosom torn by severance, that I may unfold to such a one the pain of love-desire.*

---

*The wound is the place where the Light enters you.*

---

*The spirit is like a falcon that has been captured and hooded. It sits on the wrist of this world, forgetting the vast sky. But sometimes the hood slips, and for a moment the falcon sees — and the longing to fly becomes greater than the fear of flight.*

---

*What you seek is seeking you.*

---

*This is Love: to fly heavenward, to rend every instant a hundred veils.*
