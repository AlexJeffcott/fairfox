# Glossary

- **The Architect**: Whatever intelligence designed and built the Torus. Nature unknown. Everything about it is unknown
- **The Subject**: The protagonist. No name. A child at the start, grows up over the course of the story
- **The Torus**: The vast toroidal structure where the story takes place. Large enough to support life
- **Decanting**: The Subject emerging from the artificial womb. Not birth — manufacturing
- **The Settlers**: People from prior generations living inside the Torus. Found exploits and settled for varied reasons
- **The Dead**: Bones and remains of prior generations, densest near the start, thinning deeper in
- **Quality control**: The early chambers — not real challenges, just filters to check for regressions and rejects
- **Litanies**: Principles of thinking scratched into walls by predecessors. Not puzzle solutions — epistemology. Rationalist tradition, Stoic maxims, scientific principles, philosophical razors. Some wrong. Some contradictory. The accumulated wisdom and errors of generations
- **Micro challenges**: Small, low-stakes tests woven into the prose. Teach or revisit concepts. Missing one costs something but doesn't kill you
- **Killing challenge**: High-stakes test at the end of each chapter. Draws on everything the chapter taught. Failure is death — restart the chapter as the next generation
- **The Bracer**: Worn on the Subject's forearm from the start. Dark metal, close-fitted, warm. Channels and recesses cut into its surface — intricate, deliberate. Challenges engrave new patterns into the metal: lines deepen, shapes emerge, the surface changes. The engraving is both record and key — specific patterns unlock specific passages. The growing pattern is itself a map of your path through the Torus. Different routes produce different engravings. Comparing bracers with Settlers reveals non-linear paths
