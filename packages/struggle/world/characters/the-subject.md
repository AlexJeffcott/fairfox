# The Subject

## Core
- **Role**: Protagonist
- **Age**: ~9 at decanting. The story spans years — she grows up inside the Torus. The reader ages with her
- **First appearance**: Opening scene. The corridor

## Nature

She is not special. The difference between her and the ones who didn't make it is tiny — luck, timing, a fractional edge compounded over generations. She doesn't know she's the product of an experiment. She doesn't know she's different from anyone. She's just a person.

She comes pre-loaded with aptitude. Not knowledge — she's never seen flint before — but she picks things up terrifyingly fast. Examines something for a few moments, experiments, figures it out. What would take someone else hours takes her minutes. She doesn't know this is unusual.

The whole organism has been optimised. Not just her mind — her body, reflexes, senses, endurance, immune response. She is the best version of what a human could be after generations of ruthless selection. Marginally. Not superhuman. Just the far edge of the bell curve in every direction.

## The Bracer

She wakes wearing it. Dark metal, close-fitted, warm. Channels and recesses cut into its surface — intricate, deliberate. As she progresses, challenges engrave new patterns into the metal: lines deepen, shapes emerge, the surface changes. The engraving is both record and key — specific patterns unlock specific passages.

The growing pattern is itself a map of her path through the Torus. Different routes produce different engravings. When she eventually meets Settlers, comparing bracers reveals non-linear paths — the Torus isn't a corridor and the bracers prove it.

It's one of the few things that suggests active, deliberate design. Someone placed this on her arm. It's precise, functional, waiting to be marked.

What happens when the engraving is complete is unknown.

## The Opening

A dark corridor, bones underfoot, stone on both sides. She is already moving. The passage widens, the light grows, the detritus thins. She finds a hollow worn to the shape of a body by those who came before. Sleeps.

When she wakes, the cave chamber is around her. She explores — the bracer, the flint and tinder, the wall marks, the basin. Has fire in minutes. The Torus responds to capability.

## Personality
- Quick to learn — not just intelligent, but fast. The speed is the thing
- No self-pity. This is just the world
- Grows up over the course of the story. Time passes. Years. She changes
- **Awakens with a competitive, possessive edge** — contemptuous of weakness, egotistical, angry, even megalomaniacal. This is bred into her. But it must be shown through choices and small language, never stated. "It is mine alone" — claiming the light itself as hers. The reader should feel it before they can name it

## Relationships
- **The Settlers**: Encountered late. People, not symbols. Some help her. Some might not. She may stay with them for a time
- **The Architect**: Unknown to her for most of the story. Discovery, if it happens, comes late
- **The Dead**: Bones and remains throughout the Torus. Denser early on (where the wear is heaviest), thinning out further in

## Voice Notes

She has some form of implanted language capacity — enough to function. The story doesn't stop to explain this.

- Short sentences, often fragments
- Concrete and physical
- No self-pity, no reflection early on. She acts, she doesn't narrate
- Internal voice develops as she ages and the story progresses
